From Continuous to Discrete: A No-U-Turn Sampler for Permutations

Authors: Nawaf Bou-Rabee, Zichu Wang

Quarterly of Applied Mathematics, accepted, 2026

We introduce a discrete-space analogue of the No-U-Turn sampler on the symmetric group. The method replaces continuous Hamiltonian trajectories with measure-preserving group-orbit exploration, giving a locally adaptive and reversible Markov chain Monte Carlo algorithm for distributions on permutations.

The paper develops the construction of the sampler, proves reversibility through the Gibbs self-tuning framework, and establishes mixing-time guarantees for the Mallows model under suitable assumptions.

Optimal Transport with a Density-Dependent Cost Function

Authors: Zichu Wang, Esteban G. Tabak

Submitted to Information and Inference: A Journal of the IMA, 2025

We propose a density-dependent cost function for optimal transport. In this formulation, the cost between two points is defined through a minimal-action path whose Lagrangian depends on an underlying probability distribution. This construction makes two points close only when they can be connected by paths that avoid regions of small probability.

The resulting transport geometry is therefore sensitive to the underlying data distribution and can better capture the intrinsic structure of the data. We develop a numerical framework for the corresponding data-driven optimal transport problem, with applications to clustering and matching problems.

How I Learned to Stop Worrying and Love StopGrads: Stationarity, Convergence, and a Case Study on Flow Map Learning

Authors: Mark Goldstein, Max W. Shen, Zichu Wang, Aahlad Manas Puli, Rajesh Ranganath

NeurIPS 2026 Conference Submission, 2026

Stopgrad methods are widely used in training machine learning models, but stopgrad operations can alter gradients, stationary points, and convergence guarantees of the original objective.

In this work, we introduce a stopgrad regression principle, which identifies a general template for stopgrad objectives with a closed-form characterization of stationary points. The framework unifies stopgrad objectives for flow maps, reinforcement learning, and diffusion samplers. We also provide theoretical grounding for optimizing stopgrad flow map objectives by characterizing stationary points and proving convergence results.

This paper is currently under review.
