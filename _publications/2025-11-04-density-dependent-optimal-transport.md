**Authors:** Zichu Wang, Esteban G. Tabak

*Submitted to Information and Inference: A Journal of the IMA, 2025*

We propose a density-dependent cost function for optimal transport. In this formulation, the cost between two points is defined through a minimal-action path whose Lagrangian depends on an underlying probability distribution. This construction makes two points close only when they can be connected by paths that avoid regions of small probability. The resulting transport geometry is therefore sensitive to the underlying data distribution and can better capture the intrinsic structure of the data.

We develop a numerical framework for the corresponding data-driven optimal transport problem, with applications to clustering and matching problems.
